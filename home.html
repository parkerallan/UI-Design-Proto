New to owning a pet? Or perhaps you are looking to learn more about taking care of your pet. Click the links below to learn more about each type of pet.

Cats

Cats are considered the second-most popular domestic pet in the United States. While there are over 60 unique documented cat breeds, their care and diet can be very different. However, there are some diets and practices that are generally recommended for the well-being of your fluffy family member.

Dogs

The canis familiaris, or domestic dog, is a very social animal, and owning a dog can have many mutual benefits between both dog and owner. There have been many studies throughout the last couple of centuries citing not only social benefits of having a companion and reduced feelings of loneliness by adopting a dog.

Fish

Whether saltwater or freshwater, big or small, fish have proven themselves to be the simplest pet to take care of. Fish are most peoples first pet and for good reason. They generally require very little and are regarded as beautiful animals for there bright colors and patterns

Birds

Birds come in many different sizes from Songbirds up to Parrots and often times require more upkeep than other pets. That doesn't stop most people from owning one. Birds are considered one of the most intelligent pets you can own that can be trained as well.

Small Animals

These furry critters can be anything from a Hamster, Gerbil, Rabbit, Chinchilla, or Guinea Pig. Small animals such as the Chinchilla can live up to 20 years. They typically enjoy lots of human social interaction and are playful for kids making it a good first pet for many.
